ATTRACTIONS

  • Rainbow Village
    •

    Ranbow Village is a military dependents' village that was converted into street art.As residents painted on the old house to be demolished, and triggered others to launch the "Rescue Rainbow Village" activity, the Taichung City Government rezoned the place as a park and preserved it




  • National Taichung Theater
    •

    The opera house has the first double stage stage in Taiwan. It has a large theater (2,007 seats), a medium theater (796 seats), a small theater (200 seats) and a small outdoor theater, as well as dining spaces and sky gardens.




  • Gaomei Wetlands
    •

    Gaomei Wetlands is an ecological reserve on the west coast of Taiwan. It is the coastal wetlands formed by comprehensive fresh water injection and tidal alternation.




  • Maple Garden
    •

    It is a unique recessed green space for citizens in Taichung. The Maple Garden covers an area of more than 3 hectares. It has lakes, mangroves, green lawns, and viewing bridges. It is very suitable for walking, dating, and sports. It has comfortable greenery during the day and at night. Brilliant and ever-changing lights, open so far and quickly become one of the most popular attractions in Taichung




  • The Luce Chapel
    •

    The Luce Memorial Chapel is a Christian chapel on the campus of Tunghai University in Xitun District, Taichung, Taiwan. The chapel was named in honor of the Rev. Henry W. Luce, an American missionary in China in the late 19th century and father of publisher Henry R. Luce. On April 25, 2019, the Ministry of Culture announced that it will be upgraded to a national monument.
